Scale

What the numbers
actually mean.

Numbers make distance. They allow us to understand something without having to feel it. This page tries to close that distance, not eliminate it.

17,000+
Children killed (est.)
If you read one name per second, without stopping, it would take you nearly five hours. That is one sitting. One afternoon. To say all their names once.
1 in 40
Children in Gaza injured
Gaza's pre-war child population was approximately 1 million. One in forty of those children has been injured — many with permanent, life-altering wounds.
25,000+
Children estimated orphaned
UNICEF estimates. Many are in the care of extended family members who are themselves displaced, injured, or grieving.
Unknown
Children still under rubble
Tens of thousands of people are estimated to remain beneath rubble. How many are children is not fully known. The death toll is widely considered an undercount.

All figures on this site trace back to the organizations listed below. We prioritize primary sources over aggregators. No figure here has been invented or exaggerated.

Contribute a name.

This record is incomplete. If you have a verified name — with a source you can cite — please open an issue or pull request on GitHub. We ask that every entry include at least one verifiable source and at least one detail about the child as a person, not only how they died. Accuracy matters more here than comprehensiveness.
